Grand Chocolate Souffle

Intro:
Souffle exposes every kitchen habit: whipping patience, folding lightness, serving timing. Name from French "to puff," 19th-century classic. Reputation seems forbidding but it's well-built base lifted by whipped egg whites + steady heat.

This version prioritizes flavor over height alone. Dark glossy base → center tastes like warm chocolate mousse, crown rises light and dry. Dessert that makes tables go quiet.

Ingredients:
170 g bittersweet chocolate, ~70% cacao. Valrhona/Guittard melt smooth; semisweet OK for softer edge, avoid chips.
30 g unsalted butter + extra for ramekins. Kerrygold/Plugra add flavor; salted works but reduce added salt.
50 g granulated sugar + 25 g for coating dishes. Fine cane dissolves easily; coarse sugar → heavy foam.
4 large eggs, separated. Fresh whip better; if very small, use 5.
120 ml whole milk. Gives base body; 2% acceptable, skim too thin.
12 g all-purpose flour. King Arthur dependable; neutral GF blend substitutes.
1 tsp pure vanilla extract. Nielsen-Massey excellent; vanilla bean paste works.
1 pinch fine sea salt. Table salt sharper → use less.
1/8 tsp cream of tartar. Stabilizes whites; lemon juice substitutes.
Confectioners' sugar or cocoa powder for finishing, optional.

Equipment:
4 straight-sided ramekins, 180–240 ml each
Pastry brush or paper towel for buttering
Saucepan + heatproof bowl (or double boiler) for melting
Whisk + silicone spatula for building/folding
Stand mixer or hand mixer for egg whites
Sheet pan for oven transfer

Prepare ramekins + oven:
Heat oven 200 C, rack lower-middle. Butter each ramekin thoroughly, brushing upward bottom→rim. Upward stroke helps batter grip sides while rising. Spoon sugar in, rotate to coat interior, tap excess. Bare spots → sticking; thick patches → uneven rise.

Build chocolate base:
Warm milk until steaming. Cook flour + butter in saucepan over low heat until smooth/lightly blond, whisk in hot milk. Quick roux gives structure to hold air from egg whites. Whisk until pastry-cream consistency. Off heat, add chopped chocolate, vanilla, salt; stir until glossy and fluid. Cool few minutes, whisk in egg yolks one at time. Base should feel warm not hot; too hot → cooks yolks, flattens whites later.

Whip egg whites:
Whites in perfectly clean bowl, beat until foamy. Add cream of tartar, slowly rain in sugar as whites become opaque. Gradual sugar strengthens foam without graininess. Beat to medium-stiff peaks: tip bends slightly when whisk lifts. Dull/clumpy → overwhipped, harder to fold. Slumping like loose soap bubbles → need more time. Proper whites look glossy and silky.

Fold and fill:
Stir 1/3 whites into chocolate base to lighten. Fold rest in 2 additions: spatula cuts down through center, sweeps along bottom, lifts batter over itself while rotating bowl. Preserve max air. Stop when only few faint pale streaks remain. Should look like airy mousse, not dense pudding. Spoon into ramekins, level tops, run thumb around inside rim → shallow channel. Trench helps even rise + clean crown.

Bake + judge doneness:
Ramekins on sheet pan, bake 14–17 min. Don't open oven door during first 12 min; cool air stops lift before structure sets. Done souffle stands above rim, lightly dry top, set edges, center still quivers slightly. Aroma deeply chocolaty — warm cake batter + cocoa. Poor rise → foam deflated or underwhipped. Browns too fast → oven runs hot.

Timing:
Prep: 20 min
Active: 25 min
Cool/rest: 10 min
Bake: 14–17 min
Total: ~45–50 min

Serving:
Serve immediately — clock starts when souffles leave oven. Dust tops with confectioners' sugar or cocoa, bring straight to table. Pair with softly whipped cream, crème anglaise, or raspberries for tart contrast. Each guest breaks top with spoon, releases fragrant steam from center.

Troubleshooting:
Falls fast → underbaked or oven door opened too early
Center raw → ramekins larger than expected or oven runs cool
Dense texture → whites underwhipped or overfolded
Crooked rise → ramekins unevenly buttered/sugared
Grainy base → overheated during melting

Chef's Notes:
Deeper flavor: add 1 tsp espresso powder to milk or 1 tbsp dark rum to cooled base. Winter: tiny pinch cinnamon. Summer: serve with cherries/berries. Ramekins + base can prep ahead, but whip and fold whites only just before baking. That's real secret.
