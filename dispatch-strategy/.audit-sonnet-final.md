## New Findings

### NIT — R2/R3 worked examples embedded in requirement body

- **Location:** Lines 57–59
- **Issue:** R2 and R3 include long parenthetical worked examples (full empirical sentences, specific test method names, sample output) directly inside the requirement text. The `for example:` marker makes them non-normative, but the volume of example text in a normative paragraph slightly obscures the atomic requirement (the format mandate). A skimming implementer may read the example as the required output rather than the required format.
- **Impact:** No compliance failure; the `for example:` guard is present and spec-writing rules do not prohibit illustrative examples in normative sections. Readability concern only.
- **Recommendation:** Optionally move the worked examples to a descriptive sub-block under R2/R3, keeping the normative sentence short. Not required for PASS.
