# Grand Chocolate Souffle

Souffle exposes every kitchen habit: whipping patience, folding lightness, timing. Name from French "to puff" — 19th-century showpiece. Sounds forbidding but it's well-built base + whipped egg whites + steady heat.

This version builds dark, glossy base → center tastes like warm chocolate mousse, crown rises light and dry. Dessert that silences a table.

Ingredients:
170g bittersweet chocolate, ~70% cacao. Valrhona/Guittard melt smooth; semisweet OK for softer edge, avoid chips.
30g unsalted butter + extra for ramekins. Kerrygold/Plugra add flavor; salted works but reduce added salt.
50g granulated sugar + 25g for coating dishes. Fine cane dissolves easily; coarse sugar weighs foam down.
4 large eggs, separated. Fresh whip more reliably; if very small, use 5.
120ml whole milk. Whole gives body; 2% acceptable, skim too thin.
12g all-purpose flour. King Arthur dependable; neutral GF blend can sub.
1 tsp pure vanilla extract. Nielsen-Massey excellent; vanilla bean paste works.
1 pinch fine sea salt. Table salt sharper → use less.
1/8 tsp cream of tartar. Stabilizes whites; few drops lemon juice can sub.
Confectioners' sugar or cocoa for finishing, optional.

Equipment:
4 straight-sided ramekins, 180–240ml each
Pastry brush or paper towel for even buttering
Saucepan + heatproof bowl (or double boiler) for melting
Whisk + silicone spatula for building/folding base
Stand mixer or hand mixer for egg whites
Sheet pan for oven transfer

Technique:

Prepare ramekins and oven:
Heat oven 200°C, rack lower-middle. Butter each ramekin thoroughly, brushing upward bottom→rim — upward stroke helps batter grip sides. Spoon sugar in, rotate to coat interior, tap out excess. Bare spots → sticking; thick patches → uneven rise.

Build chocolate base:
Warm milk until steaming. Cook flour + butter over low heat until smooth/lightly blond, whisk in hot milk. Quick roux gives structure to hold air from whites. Whisk until pastry-cream consistency. Off heat, add chopped chocolate, vanilla, salt; stir until glossy and fluid. Cool few minutes, whisk in yolks one at a time. Base should feel warm not hot — too hot cooks yolks and later flattens whites.

Whip egg whites:
Whites in clean bowl, beat until foamy. Add cream of tartar, slowly rain in sugar as whites turn opaque. Gradual sugar strengthens foam without graininess. Beat to medium-stiff peaks: tip bends slightly when whisk lifts. Dull/clumpy → overwhipped, harder to fold. Slumping like loose soap bubbles → need more time. Proper whites look glossy and silky.

Fold and fill:
Stir 1/3 whites into chocolate base to lighten. Fold rest in 2 additions with spatula: cut down center, sweep along bottom, lift batter over itself while rotating bowl. Preserve max air. Stop when only faint pale streaks remain. Batter should look like airy mousse, not dense pudding. Spoon into ramekins, level tops, run thumb around inside rim → shallow channel helps even rise and clean crown.

Bake and judge doneness:
Ramekins on sheet pan, bake 14–17 min. Don't open oven during first 12 min — cool air blast can stop lift before structure sets. Done souffle: stands above rim, lightly dry top, set edges, center still quivers. Aroma deeply chocolaty like warm cake batter + cocoa. Poor rise → foam deflated or underwhipped. Browns too fast → oven runs hot.

Timing:
Prep: 20 min
Active: 25 min
Cooling/resting: 10 min
Bake: 14–17 min
Total: ~45–50 min

Serving:
Serve immediately — clock starts when souffles leave oven. Dust tops with confectioners' sugar or cocoa, straight to table. Pair with softly whipped cream, crème anglaise, or raspberries for tart contrast. Each guest breaks top with spoon, releasing fragrant steam from center.

Troubleshooting:
Falls fast → underbaked or oven opened too early
Center raw → ramekins larger than expected or oven runs cool
Dense texture → whites underwhipped or overfolded
Crooked rise → ramekins unevenly buttered/sugared
Grainy base → overheated during melting

Chef's Notes:
Deeper flavor: add 1 tsp espresso powder to milk or 1 tbsp dark rum to cooled base. Winter: tiny pinch cinnamon. Summer: serve with cherries/berries. Can prep ramekins + base ahead but whip and fold whites only before baking — that's real secret.
