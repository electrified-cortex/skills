# Grand Chocolate Soufflé

## Intro

Soufflé exposes kitchen habits: whipping, folding, timing. French "to puff" — 19th-century showpiece. Well-built base + whipped whites + steady heat.

This version: dark glossy base → center = warm chocolate mousse, crown rises light/dry.

## Ingredients

- 170 g bittersweet chocolate, ~70% cacao. Valrhona/Guittard; semisweet OK, no chips.
- 30 g unsalted butter + more for ramekins. Kerrygold/Plugra; salted works (less added salt).
- 50 g sugar + 25 g for coating. Fine cane; coarse → heavy foam.
- 4 large eggs, separated. Fresh = reliable whip; small eggs → use 5.
- 120 ml whole milk. Body for base; 2% OK, skim too thin.
- 12 g AP flour. GF blend substitutes.
- 1 tsp vanilla extract. Bean paste works.
- Pinch fine sea salt. Table salt sharper → use less.
- 1/8 tsp cream of tartar. Stabilizes whites; lemon juice alt.
- Confectioners' sugar/cocoa for finishing (optional).

## Equipment

- 4 straight-sided ramekins, 180–240 ml
- Pastry brush for even buttering
- Saucepan + heatproof bowl for melting
- Whisk + silicone spatula
- Stand/hand mixer for whites
- Sheet pan for oven transfer

## Technique

### Ramekins + oven

200°C, lower-middle rack. Butter ramekins upward (helps batter grip). Sugar coat → rotate → tap excess. Bare spots → sticking. Thick patches → uneven rise.

### Chocolate base

Steam milk. Flour + butter over low → smooth, lightly blond → whisk in hot milk. Roux = structure for air. Whisk to pastry cream consistency. Off heat: add chocolate, vanilla, salt → stir glossy/fluid. Cool, whisk in yolks one at a time. Warm not hot — hot → cooks yolks → flattens whites.

### Whites

Clean bowl, beat foamy. Add cream of tartar, rain sugar slowly. Gradual = strong foam, not grainy. Medium-stiff peaks (tip bends). Dull/clumpy = overwhipped. Slumping = more time. Target: glossy, silky.

### Fold + fill

1/3 whites → stir into base (lighten). Rest in 2 additions: cut down, sweep bottom, lift over, rotate. Preserve max air. Stop at faint pale streaks. Airy mousse, not dense pudding. Fill ramekins, level, thumb channel around rim → even rise + clean crown.

### Bake

Sheet pan, 14–17 min. No door first 12 min (cool air stops lift). Done: above rim, dry top, set edges, center quivers. Aroma: deeply chocolaty. Poor rise → deflated/underwhipped. Browns fast → oven hot.

## Timing

Prep 20 min · Active 25 min · Rest 10 min · Bake 14–17 min · Total ~45–50 min

## Serving

Serve immediately — clock starts at oven exit. Dust sugar/cocoa, straight to table. Pairs: whipped cream, crème anglaise, raspberries. Guest breaks top → fragrant steam.

## Troubleshooting

- Falls fast → underbaked / door opened early
- Raw center → ramekins too large / oven cool
- Dense → whites underwhipped / overfolded
- Crooked rise → uneven buttering
- Grainy base → overheated

## Notes

Deeper flavor: 1 tsp espresso powder in milk or 1 tbsp dark rum in base. Winter: pinch cinnamon. Summer: cherries/berries. Ramekins + base prep ahead — whites only before baking. Secret: timing.
